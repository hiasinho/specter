how it works

Three commands. That's it.

01

Initialize

Point Specter at your docs directory. A single YAML config defines what gets synced and what stays local.

specter init
02

Push

Specter hashes every document, detects what changed, and syncs only the delta. Branches get their own namespace automatically.

specter push
03

Collaborate

Propose changes, review diffs, pull updates. Your team and your agents all work from the same source of truth.

specter propose
features

Designed for the way teams actually work

Not a wiki. Not a CMS. A sync primitive you can build workflows on top of.

SHA-256 conflict detection

Every document is hashed before sync. Specter catches divergence before it becomes a conflict, not after.

Branch namespacing

Your git branch is your namespace. Feature branches get isolated document states. Merging branches merges knowledge.

Full document history

Every push creates a numbered revision. Diff any two revisions, restore old versions, trace who changed what and when.

Propose & review workflow

Stage a proposal before it goes live. Reviewers see a clean diff. Approve or reject from the CLI or the dashboard.

Agent-native API

Machine-readable output on every command. Pipe Specter into your CI, your LLM agent, your custom toolchain. It just works.

Invite & access control

Add collaborators to your project with a single command. Token-based auth keeps access manageable as teams scale.

the cli

Every workflow,
from the terminal

No dashboard required. Specter's CLI covers the full lifecycle: init, push, pull, diff, status, propose, review, history, and invite. Composable, scriptable, fast.

specter status — see local vs remote in one glance
specter diff — unified diff for any document
specter history — full revision log per document
specter invite — add teammates by email
